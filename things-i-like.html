A few things before you go on reading...

  • I just like these things okay i'm no expert on any of em
  • These are all personal opinions, i'm not saying anything here is objectively good
  • I try to list things in here regardless of their fanbases, no matter how cringe they get
  • I try to list things in here regardless of whoever made them
    • Because of that, you might see names like C. Dodgson and R. Wagner because i think their works are very entertaining or important, in spite of the questionable stuff surrounding them.
    • That being said, there should be names like Gus & Eddy that i put in here because they make good stuff AND they seem like swell folk too even tho i've never known them personally
  • I try to come back and update this page often because thinking about things i like and why i like them makes me feel nice

Thing Remarks
Aim and Ignite (youtube|spotify|bandcamp)
  • 2009 album by fun.
  • I dont think i talk about this album enough
  • I thought i just hung on to this album because of nostalgia but it still hits when i come back to it
Airmech (pre-strike)
  • Probably the first action rts with units type game i fell in love with back in 2012(?) i think. and it was free!
  • You got to play as a pilot in a plane that transforms into a robot that commands units how cool was that
  • Maybe i've become old and jaded but the game as of 2021/09/25 is not as great it once was
    • some of the coolest stuff like stat customizations and pilots got locked away behind the airmech wastelands paywall
    • and what's left in the free airmech strike just ain't it
    • dev team is silent and is effectively abandonware
Animals (youtube|spotify|bandcamp)
  • 2008 album by TTNG
  • Ok i've taken some time to put into words why i love this album very very much, here's why
  • there's a 2018 acoustic version of this album that TTNG made as the 10 yr anniversary and i love it just as much (youtube|spotify|bandcamp)
Ben Levin's stuff
  • The man is somehow a weirder Bill Wurtz if that's even possible
  • He makes incredible things on his youtube channel about music and life and stuff with weird blender animations it's great
Bill Wurtz's stuff

Firefly
  • TV series by Joss Whedon about a ragtag team of spaceship crew folk tryna get by

Git and Github
  • Git is a tool typically used by software programmer type folk for working on projects collaboratively while Github is a company that provides hosting for git repositories
  • I've always thought version control solutions were fascinating and i wish there was something as good as Git for making music together cross DAW. idk how anyone would go about doing it but i can dream
    • i suppose Splice comes close to what i'm imagining of but it's nowhere near free and while it does support a good amount of DAWs it doesn't support the ones my friends mainly use
  • I'm always a sucker for free software
  • This website's hosted using Github Pages!
  • I've found that i've learnt how Git is structured and what git can do best while using Git command line rather than Git gui or Github Desktop

The Mysterious Benedict Society
  • A book series by Trenton Lee Stewart
  • Think Lemony Snicket's series of unfortunate events even down to the appreciation of literacy but with less frustrating adults
  • At the time of my typing this, there's an adaptation coming out on disney+
    • Trailer doesn't look like it's capturing characters in the book 1:1 but i'm open for alternate interpretation up to a point
Neil Cicierega's stuff
  • oh my god what hasn't he done? or at least tried? he's like the internet thing maker before internet thing makers
    • okay but he's made music under his own name and lemon demon, animations, websites and god knows what else
  • super fond of Mouth Moods, his cursed mashup album (youtube)
Nicky Case's stuff
  • I know them for making cool articles and webgames on stuff that makes me think sometimes
  • Check out their website there's some seriously cool stuff there, i particularly like the evolution of trust
OneShot
  • A really great pixel art puzzle rpg where... yeah go into it blind if you can
  • Think UNDERTALE with less action but the protag has an actual personality
  • Great soundtrack too, by Nightmargin, with additional tracks by Eliza Velasquez and GIRakaCHEEZER
Outer Wilds
  • Singleplayer space puzzle exploration game by Mobius Digital, and not the Outer Worlds by other company
  • Go into it blind if you want to try it! only look at a trailer and spoiler free reviews if you must!
  • I've had no problems at all and found it an absolutely incredible experience, there's so much charm in the whole solar system
  • Great soundtrack by Andrew Prahlow too (youtube)
  • Some valid criticisms i've seen however:
    • 6 degrees of freedom might make for big motion sickness, and i've seen a couple claims of bad controls
    • Some players find the timing/pace of how puzzles/story progress not good
    • Some weird stuff about Mobius Digital initially releasing exclusively on epic games, backers who kickstarted for xbox/steam releases had to wait longer than promised
  • Once again i cannot stress enough how much I adore this game. It's just one of those things that gives me something to believe in again.
Over the Garden Wall
  • An animated miniseries by Patrick McHale where two kids get lost in the woods and try to get home
  • I always say it's the best thing i know of that's come out on Cartoon Network, and i think i've seen all of the best of what CN has to offer
Passionflower (youtube|spotify)
  • 2011 Single by Jon Gomm
  • Great song and performance and i know for a fact it inspires a lot of my guitarist friends
The Phantom Tollbooth
  • A book by Norton Juster
  • Kid is bored, gets a tollbooth and goes on an adventure and resolves conflicts n stuff
  • Think alice in wonderland but it's not alice and it's not wonderland
PIXAR movies
  • Yeah they're cool, nuff said
The SCP Foundation
  • A collaborative writing project by users about a fictional shadowy organization trying to secure, contain and protect paranormal type stuff
  • I think the quality control is really good, but hey what do i know about writing
  • One of the better things to come out of 4chan's /x/ board and spawned many more works inspired by it not limited to but including
    • the piece of media i feel that shoved SCP into the limelight a lot more: Regalis's videogame SCP containment breach
    • Lord Bung's animated series Confinement
    • Evan Royalty's short films
  • Something about this shared universe of sorts is just so fun to read
Shut up and sit down
  • Boardgame reviewer/journalist folk who are also pretty funny
  • i mainly watch stuff on their youtube channel but they have great stuff on their website too
Treasure Planet
  • 2002 animated sci-fi adaptation of RL Stevenson's novel Treasure Island
  • I watched it when i was very young and could well be the reason i like scifi and ships and stuff. It holds up very very well when i rewatched it in my adolescence as well.
  • I love this film to bits and i think it's a shame Disney screwed over its release
